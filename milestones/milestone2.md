## Reflection

### 1. What went well during this unit?

Our group made significant progress planning the College Football Scout Helper application. We identified the major pages required for the application, designed multiple wireframes using Figma, Stitch AI and PowerPoint, and refined them into a consistent user interface. We also completed a six-table Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) that models how users, players, teams, player statistics, scout lists, and tryout invitations interact with one another. Having these planning documents completed gives us a strong foundation before we begin implementing the application.

### 2. What were some challenges your group faced in this unit?

One challenge was deciding which pages were essential for the project and how they should support the user stories. We initially considered adding a dashboard, but after reviewing our user stories and database schema, we realized it was unnecessary for the current scope. Another challenge was designing a database that was simple enough for the project while still supporting all required features such as player scouting, statistics, and tryout invitations. Creating consistent wireframes that matched the overall design was also time-consuming but worthwhile.

### 3. What additional support will you need in upcoming units as you continue to work on your final project?

As we move into development, we will need guidance on implementing authentication, connecting the React frontend with the Express backend, and integrating PostgreSQL with our API endpoints. We also want to ensure our application follows good full-stack development practices, including responsive design, database normalization, and clean RESTful API implementation. Finally, we will need support integrating our AI Assistant feature and deploying the completed application successfully.
